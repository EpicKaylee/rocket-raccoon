C++

C++ is a high-level, general-purpose programming language created by Bjarne Stroustrup. It was developed with the purpose of building upon the well-known language 'C', and its name was derived from this idea. Thus, the name 'C++'.

Level of Difficulty:

Students have reported that this programming language is difficult to understand. It has been rated as intermediate in terms of its difficulty according to LinkedIn. Following the conducted interviews, we have found that the ratings on difficulty range from 6 to 8 on a scale of 1 to 10.

Popularity:

Based on the survey, C++ is known for its capabilities, although it is tough; some students are familiar with using it.

How was this language derived?

Creator: Bjarne Stroustrop.

Released: 15 October 1985.

From: Dell Laborataries, USA.

Its name is derived from a previous product of Dell Laboratories, 'C,' with the addition of classes, object-oriented support, and other functional features. Thus, the name 'C++' came into fruition.

PROCESS OF LEARNING

I took a course on C++ during week 5 to learn more about it. Although I have experience with simpler programming languages like Python, I had never worked with an intermediate language like this one before. I found that both languages share similar concepts; for example, the 'print' function in Python is equivalent to "std::cout>>" in C++. C++ is definitely more complex, but its concepts seem more flexible in their usage.
